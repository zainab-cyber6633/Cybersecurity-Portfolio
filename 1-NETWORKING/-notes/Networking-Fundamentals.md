# Networking Fundamentals

## Overview

Networking is the process of connecting two or more devices to share data, resources, and communication services. It is one of the fundamental concepts in Information Technology and Cybersecurity.

---

## Learning Objectives

After completing this topic, you will be able to:

- Define networking.
- Explain the importance of networking.
- Identify different types of networks.
- Understand basic networking devices.
- Recognize common network topologies.

---

## What is Networking?

Networking is the process of connecting multiple devices so they can communicate and exchange information.

Examples of network devices include:

- Computers
- Servers
- Routers
- Switches
- Printers
- Smartphones

---

## Why Networking is Important

Networking provides many advantages, including:

- Resource Sharing
- Internet Access
- Communication
- Centralized Management
- Remote Access
- Data Sharing

---

## Types of Networks

### PAN (Personal Area Network)

A Personal Area Network connects devices within a short distance.

Example:

Bluetooth connection between a mobile phone and wireless earbuds.

---

### LAN (Local Area Network)

A Local Area Network connects devices inside a home, office, or school.

Example:

Computer Lab

---

### MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)

A Metropolitan Area Network connects multiple LANs within a city.

Example:

University campuses connected together.

---

### WAN (Wide Area Network)

A Wide Area Network connects networks over large geographical areas.

Example:

The Internet.

---

## Network Topologies

### Star Topology

All devices connect to a central switch.

Advantages

- Easy to troubleshoot
- High performance

Disadvantages

- Switch failure disconnects the network.

---

### Bus Topology

All devices share one communication cable.

Advantages

- Low cost

Disadvantages

- Difficult troubleshooting

---

### Ring Topology

Devices form a circular connection.

Advantages

- Predictable communication

Disadvantages

- Failure of one device can interrupt communication.

---

### Mesh Topology

Every device connects with multiple devices.

Advantages

- High reliability

Disadvantages

- Expensive
- Complex configuration

---

## Common Networking Devices

### Router

Connects different networks.

### Switch

Connects devices within the same LAN.

### Hub

Broadcasts data to all connected devices.

### Firewall

Protects networks by filtering traffic.

### Access Point

Provides wireless connectivity.

---

## Basic Networking Terms

### Node

Any device connected to a network.

### Protocol

A set of communication rules.

Examples:

- TCP
- UDP
- HTTP
- HTTPS
- FTP
- DNS

### Packet

A small unit of data transmitted across a network.

### Bandwidth

The maximum amount of data transmitted over a network.

### Latency

The delay between sending and receiving data.
